Investment Implications
Rate cuts pushed further out — duration repricing
Headline PCE jumped to 3,5% in April. Markets should price fewer cuts for 2026. Pressure on fixed income duration.
Growing internal division — FOMC more hawkish than it appears
Three hawks voted against the easing bias in April. The official statement softens an internally more restrictive stance.
Leadership transition — communication risk
Powell exits May 15, Warsh incoming. Possible shift in tone and policy signaling. Key variable to monitor closely.
Higher for longer as base case
While energy keeps pushing headline inflation up, the Fed has no room to ease. Short rates remain elevated.
Long-term anchoring intact — no de-anchoring yet
Long-term inflation expectations remain consistent with 2%. Chronic inflation is not the base case for now.
